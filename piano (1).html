PIANO

The piano is a stringed keyboard instrument in which the strings are struck by wooden hammers that are coated with a softer material. The English word "piano" as used for this musical instrument is a shortened form of pianoforte, the Italian term for the early 1700s versions of the instrument, which in turn derives from clavicembalo col piano e forte



Developed: Early 18th century
Inventor: Bartolomeo Cristofori
