XYLOPHONE

The xylophone (from Ancient Greek ξύλον (xúlon) 'wood', and φωνή (phōnḗ) 'sound, voice';[1][2] lit. 'sound of wood') is a musical instrument in the percussion family that consists of wooden bars struck by mallets. The term xylophone may be used generally, to include all such instruments such as the marimba, balafon and even the semantron.




Developed: 1886
Inventor: Albert Roth.
